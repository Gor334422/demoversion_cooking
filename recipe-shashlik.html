Shashlik

Juicy meat on coals, marinated in aromatic spices - perfect meat for a picnic

👨‍🍳 Instructor: Arthur Sarkisyan ⏱️ Time: 60 minutes (+ marinating) 👥 Servings: 6 ⭐ Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

For the Meat:

  • Beef (pieces 3x3 cm): 1.5 kg
  • Onion: 5 medium onions
  • Apple cider vinegar: 250 ml
  • Vegetable oil: 100 ml
  • Water: 1 cup

Spices:

  • Paprika: 1 tablespoon
  • Black pepper: 1 teaspoon
  • Coriander: 1 teaspoon
  • Cumin: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Salt: to taste
  • Garlic: 4 cloves
  • Fresh herbs: parsley, cilantro

For Serving:

  • Lemon: 2 pcs
  • Tomatoes: 3 pcs
  • Onion (greens): 1 bunch

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Preparing the Marinade

Cut the onion into half-rings. In a large bowl, mix vinegar, vegetable oil, water, paprika, black pepper, coriander, cumin, salt, garlic pressed through a press and fresh herbs.

2

Marinating the Meat

Cut the meat into pieces 3x3 cm in size. Place meat and onion in layers in a deep dish, alternating meat and onion. Pour over the marinade so that the meat is completely covered. Cover with a lid and leave in the refrigerator for 6-8 hours or overnight.

3

Preparing the Skewers

Soak wooden skewers in water for 30 minutes before use (this will prevent them from burning). Take the meat out of the marinade, shaking off excess marinade slightly.

4

Stringing on Skewers

String the meat onto skewers alternating pieces of meat and onion from the marinade. Each skewer should have 4-5 pieces of meat. Press the pieces tightly together.

5

Grilling

Light the coals until they are light gray. Place the grill 10-15 cm above the coals. Place the shashlik on the grill. Grill for 15-20 minutes, turning every 3-4 minutes for even cooking. The meat should be juicy inside and with a golden crust outside.

6

Serving

Place the finished shashlik on a plate. Serve hot with sliced tomatoes, lemon and herbs. Traditionally served with lavash or flatbread. Squeeze lemon directly on the meat before eating.

💡 Tips and Recommendations

  • Meat Choice: Beef of the first category, pork or lamb is ideal. You can combine different types of meat.
  • Marinating Time: The longer the meat marinates, the softer it will be. Minimum 2 hours, optimally 8-12 hours.
  • Coal Temperature: Use coals from hardwood (oak, beech) for better taste.
  • Checking Readiness: The meat is ready when the juice released when pressed is clear.
  • Alternative: Can be cooked in the oven at 200°C for 30-40 minutes on a baking sheet.
