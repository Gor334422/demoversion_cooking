Khinkali

Delicate Georgian dumplings with juicy meat filling - a true culinary wonder

👨‍🍳 Instructor: Karine Davtyan ⏱️ Time: 90 minutes 👥 Servings: 4 (12 pieces) ⭐ Difficulty: High

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • Flour: 3 cups
  • Water: 1 cup warm
  • Salt: 1 teaspoon
  • Egg: 1 pc

For the Filling:

  • Ground beef: 600 g
  • Onion: 3 medium onions
  • Broth: 200 ml (beef or chicken)
  • Spices (coriander, cumin): 1 teaspoon
  • Black pepper: to taste
  • Salt: to taste
  • Fresh coriander (greens): 1 bunch

For Cooking:

  • Water: 3 liters
  • Salt: 2 tablespoons

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Preparing the Dough

On a clean table, pour out the flour, add salt. Make a depression in the center. Pour in warm water and beaten egg. Gradually knead the dough, gathering flour from the sides. Knead for 10-15 minutes until elastic. Cover with a towel and let rest for 20 minutes.

2

Preparing the Filling

Finely chop the onion. Mix ground beef, onion, spices, salt and pepper. Important: the filling should be semi-raw, as it will cook in the boiling broth inside the khinkali. Add a small amount of warm broth to the meat for juiciness.

3

Rolling and Cutting the Dough

Divide the dough into 12 equal parts. Roll each part into a round flatbread 3-4 mm thick, about 10 cm in diameter. You can use a glass to cut even circles.

4

Shaping Khinkali

On each dough circle, place a tablespoon of filling. Gather the dough edges to the center, pinching them between fingers, forming a purse. The top should be tightly closed. Transfer to a flour-dusted board.

5

Cooking Khinkali

Bring salted water to a boil in a large pot. Carefully drop khinkali one by one. When they float, cook for another 3-4 minutes. Remove with a slotted spoon and place on a plate.

6

Serving

Place hot khinkali on a plate, drizzle with spiced ghee. Sprinkle with fresh coriander. Serve immediately while khinkali are still hot. Bite from the end, enjoying the juicy meat broth inside.

💡 Tips and Recommendations

  • Main Secret: The filling should be sufficiently liquid, as the juice turns into broth inside the khinkali during cooking.
  • Closing Khinkali: The edges must be hermetically sealed, otherwise the filling will leak out. You can use the dough edges to tie them in a knot.
  • Dough: It should be soft and elastic, not hard. Excess flour will make khinkali tough.
  • Shaping Technique: Practice is required. It may be difficult at first, but after 2-3 attempts it will work.
  • Spiced Butter: Melt ghee, add ground black pepper, salt and paprika for serving.
